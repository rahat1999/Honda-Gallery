Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

Bear in mind that if the helmet did its job most people would tell you that they did not even hit their head, or did not hit their head that hard. But if you can see marks on the shell or measure any foam crush at all, replace the helmet

Take your bike around the block and make sure it is safe to ride.
  • First of all, your tires are going to be deflated, it is normal after long periods of time on storage.
  • Make sure your brakes work.
  • Your chain will probably need re-lubrication (or even replacement).

    • When riding, always:
  • Obey All Traffic Laws. A bicycle is a vehicle and you're a driver.
  • Yield to Traffic When Appropriate.
  • Be Predictable.
  • Stay Alert at All Times
  • Look Before Turning.
  • Watch for Parked Cars.
